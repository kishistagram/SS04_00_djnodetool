# Product Vision

## Product Name

NodeMix Canvas

## One-line Description

NodeMix Canvas is a node-based DJ and music arrangement tool that lets users organize, connect, and transition between tracks visually on a free-form canvas instead of managing music only as a vertical playlist.

## Problem

Most music playlist tools organize tracks as vertical lists.

This structure is useful for playback order, but it does not match how many people think about music.

When choosing music, people often think through associations:

- mood
- memory
- genre
- era
- tempo
- place
- season
- color
- texture
- personal experience
- relationship between songs

A vertical playlist hides these relationships.

## Core Insight

Music selection is not always linear.

A user may want to move from one song to another because:

- the mood is similar
- the rhythm feels compatible
- the memory is connected
- the genre contrast is interesting
- the transition creates surprise
- the two tracks share an emotional color

This product treats music selection as a graph, not a list.

## Vision

The product allows users to place tracks as nodes on a canvas and connect them with transition edges.

Each node represents a track.

Each edge represents a relationship or transition between tracks.

Over time, the canvas becomes a visual map of the user's musical world.

## MVP Direction

The first version should not try to become a full DJ application.

The MVP should prove only one core idea:

A user can arrange tracks spatially, connect them visually, and play a simple transition between connected tracks.

## Long-term Direction

In the future, the product may support:

- AI-assisted transitions
- natural language instructions
- mood-based arrangement
- color/image/shape-based music editing
- generated music workflows
- DJ performance mode
- music knowledge graphs
- collaborative music maps
- sampling and influence visualization

## Product Principles

- Visual first
- Spatial, not vertical
- Simple interactions
- Understandable structure
- User controls the music map
- AI assists later, but does not define the first product
- The data model should be clear and extensible
- The prototype should prioritize learning and architecture over feature quantity
